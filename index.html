Synopsis

The Kite Runner tells the story of Amir, a young boy from Kabul, whose closest friend is Hassan, the son of his father's servant. Against the backdrop of a changing Afghanistan, from the fall of the monarchy through the Soviet invasion, the mass exodus of refugees to Pakistan and the United States, and the rise of the Taliban regime, The Kite Runner tells a story of friendship, betrayal, and the price of loyalty.

It is about the power of reading, the price of betrayal, and the possibility of redemption. It is also about the bonds between fathers and sons, and the power of their lies. The story moves from Afghanistan in the final days of the monarchy to the present, covering the country's tragic recent history.
